Product Management

Leading Development Teams to Meet Business Needs

A product manager is the root cause when development efforts succeed or fail; they are the final say on what goes out the door.

An effective product manager must hold the following characteristics.

  • The most important part of a product managers job is to identify and prioritize development efforts.
  • They must serve as the buffer between the business and the dev teams. They should be able to effectively engage with stakeholders
  • They must understand the business needs of any project, being able to vet, document and story requirements.
  • When necessary they must not be afraid to push back on the business when misalignment between objectives or constraints occur.

Process Improvement

Thinking Beyond Where We are Right Now and Improving Execution

When looking to improve an existing process, we can ask the following questions.

  • Why does this process exist? What is the end goal?
  • Is the process done manually?
  • How is workflow handled between parties?
  • Can relevant stakeholders be kept abreast of the process?
  • What controls are in place?
  • Are there parrallel processes already in place?

Further, when designing a solution to remediate any pain points we make sure to cover the following:

  • Can we automate inputs, outputs, or processing?
  • Can an upstream process negate the need for this process?
  • What is the development resource requirement?
  • What are the downstream impacts? What are the upstream sources?
  • If we change the process, what change management is required?
